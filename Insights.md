# Key Findings
* Departments with higher employee counts also show higher total salary cost.
* HR department has the largest workforce, which explains its higher salary budget.
* Sales department has the highest average salary, reflecting specialized technical roles.
* Sales department has the lowest employee count but salary cost is second lowest, Finance has lowest salary cost.

# Business Interpretation
* Total salary cost is primarily driven by the number of employees rather than salary imbalance.
* Higher HR salaries indicate skill-based compensation rather than inefficiency.
* Salary structure appears proportional to workforce distribution across departments.

# Business Risks
* Rapid growth in headcount without productivity tracking could increase costs.
* Over-dependence on HR talent may create operational risk if retention is not managed.

# Recommendations
* Monitor department-wise productivity alongside headcount growth.
- Ensure salary increases are aligned with role complexity and performance.
- Periodically review workforce distribution to control long-term costs.
